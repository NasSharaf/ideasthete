My name is Nasir Sharaf. I am a maker, hacker, engineer and developer.

I graduated from the University of Pittsburgh with a bachelors in mechanical engineer. Around this time I had an internship with DTE Energy. Because of that internship, I was hired by Siemens Building Technologies as an Energy Engineer where I learned a lot about controls, energy conservation, and construction. I also realized how important computers were becoming and how much untapped potential there was even in a high-tech industry like controls. So I went and got my masters in computer science from Oakland University. Most of my research was in the cybersecurity of industrial control systems.

On a personal note, I'm interested in biomimicry - the idea that we should engineer systems based on mimicking nature. That's why my master's thesis was on artificial immune systems and why I have an interest in artificial intelligence. I like reading philosophy, making art, and playing guitar. I also love martial arts and have done karate, taekwondo, judo, ving tsun (wing chun) kung fu, and a little bit of boxing.
